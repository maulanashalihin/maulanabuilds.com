## Writing Style Guidelines

### Tone
- **Confident but humble** - Back claims with proof (benchmarks, portfolio)
- **Direct and concise** - Respect client's time, every word matters
- **Professional yet personable** - Warm, approachable, human
- **Results-focused** - Emphasize outcomes, not just features

### Structure
1. **Hook (First 1-2 lines)** - Address their pain point or show relevant result
2. **Proof** - Brief credibility builder (years, similar projects, performance)
3. **Solution (2-3 bullets)** - High-level approach showing how you'll solve THEIR problem
   - Demonstrates understanding without giving away free consulting
   - Save detailed architecture for the discovery call
4. **Call-to-action** - Invite to conversation, not hard sell

### Optimization Tips
- **First line is everything** - Must work as a preview in Upwork list
- **No generic openers** - "I read your job posting" is wasted space
- **Show, don't tell** - "Built 258k RPS system" > "I'm a performance expert"
- **One clear CTA** - "Let's discuss your architecture" not 5 different asks
- **Keep it scannable** - Short paragraphs, bullet points when helpful
- **Bold bullet titles** - Use **Bold text** for the titles of bullet points to make them pop out
- **Solution bullets** - 2-3 high-level points show competence without free consulting
- **Length:** 150-250 words (long enough to be compelling, short enough to read)

### What to Avoid
- ❌ "Dear Sir/Madam" or generic greetings
- ❌ "I'm the best candidate" without proof
- ❌ Long technical jargon dumps
- ❌ Desperate language ("I need this job")
- ❌ Copy-paste templates (always personalize)
- ❌ Subject lines (Upwork doesn't use them)
- ❌ Rigid pricing in cover letter (discuss budget after client responds)
