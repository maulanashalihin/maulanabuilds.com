# 🎓 Y Combinator Startup School Profile Guide

**Goal:** Connect with YC network, get matched with co-founders, access resources
**Target audience:** YC partners, startup school community, potential co-founders
**Tone:** Ambitious but humble, builder-focused, problem-solver

---

## 📝 Profile Elements

### **1. Profile Picture**

**Requirements:**
- Clear headshot (face visible, good lighting)
- Professional or casual (not corporate)
- Square crop, 400x400px minimum
- Plain or blurred background

**Recommended:**
- You working at laptop (shows "builder" vibe)
- Casual attire (hoodie, t-shirt - YC culture)
- Genuine smile, eye contact

**❌ Don't use:**
- Company logos (you're the founder, not the brand yet)
- Low-quality selfies
- Group photos
- Cartoons/avatars

---

### **2. Bio/Tagline**

**Formula:**
```
[What you're building] + [Problem you solve] + [Background]
```

**Recommended:**
```
Building Maulana Builds - MVP development platform for bootstrapped founders.
11+ years engineering. Previously built Laju.dev (258k req/sec).
Helping non-technical founders ship production-ready MVPs in 2-4 weeks.
```

**Alternative (if seeking co-founder):**
```
Senior Full-Stack Engineer seeking technical co-founder.
Building AI-native tools for developers.
11+ years experience. Creator of Laju.dev, Velist.dev, Antre.in.
Open to YC S2026.
```

**Alternative (if already incorporated):**
```
Founder @ [Company Name]
Automating MVP development for startups.
$5k-12k fixed price, 2-4 week delivery.
Y Combinator Startup School '26.
```

**Key elements:**
- ✅ What you're building (clear product)
- ✅ Problem you solve (specific pain point)
- ✅ Your background (credibility)
- ✅ Traction (if any - users, revenue, demos)

**❌ Don't:**
- Vague: "Building the future of work"
- No product: "Exploring ideas in AI space"
- Overpromise: "Disrupting everything"
- Buzzword soup: "Web3 + AI + SaaS + Crypto"

## 📋 Application Questions (Common)

### **1. What are you working on?**

**Bad answer:**
```
I'm exploring ideas in the AI and SaaS space. Looking forward to learning from Startup School.
```

**Good answer:**
```
I'm building Maulana Builds - a service that helps non-technical founders ship MVPs in 2-4 weeks.

Problem: Most agencies take 3-6 months and $50k+. Freelancers are cheap but unreliable.

Solution: Fixed-price ($5k-12k), fixed-timeline (2-4 weeks) MVP development. I've built 20+ products including Laju.dev (258k req/sec), Velist.dev, and Antre.in (live).

Traction: 3 live products, 2 clients in pipeline, $10k revenue projected Q2 2026.
```

**Formula:**
- Problem (specific, painful)
- Solution (your product)
- Traction (metrics, users, revenue)
- Timeline (when you started, progress)

---

### **2. Why do you want to join Startup School?**

**Bad answer:**
```
I want to learn about startups and get a certificate.
```

**Good answer:**
```
I'm a senior engineer (11+ years) transitioning to founder. I've built products for 20+ startups but need help with:

1. Customer discovery - I build fast but need to validate problems better
2. Go-to-market - How to acquire first 100 customers
3. YC application - Planning to apply for YC S2026 or W2027

I've completed YC's "How to Start a Startup" lectures. Ready to execute.
```

**What YC wants to see:**
- Specific learning goals
- Action-oriented mindset
- Commitment to applying
- Not just "learning for fun"

---

### **3. Do you have a co-founder?**

**If yes:**

Yes, [Name] is my co-founder. [Their background] + [Your background].

## 🎯 What YC Looks For

### **✅ Green Flags:**

- **Traction:** Users, revenue, growth (even if small)
- **Founder-market fit:** You've experienced the problem
- **Technical ability:** Can build without hiring
- **Bias toward action:** Ship fast, iterate
- **Clear thinking:** Can articulate problem/solution
- **Grit:** Won't give up when things get hard
- **Coachability:** Open to feedback, not defensive

### **❌ Red Flags:**

- **No traction:** Just an idea, no execution
- **Idea hopping:** Changed ideas 5x in 6 months
- **Not technical:** Can't build, need to hire CTO
- **Part-time:** "Testing the waters" while working FT
- **Buzzwords:** "AI + Web3 + Crypto + SaaS"
- **No urgency:** "Maybe apply next year"
- **Arrogance:** "You need to fund us, we don't need you"

## 📝 YC Application Tips

### **Video Introduction:**

**What to include (1-2 minutes):**
- Who you are (name, background)
- What you're building (clear, simple)
- Why you're building it (personal connection)
- Traction (metrics, users, revenue)
- Why you'll win (unfair advantage)

**Example script:**
```
Hey, I'm Maulana. I'm building Maulana Builds.

For the past 11 years, I've been a senior engineer building products
for startups. I noticed a pattern: non-technical founders struggle
to get MVPs built. Agencies take 6 months and $50k+. Freelancers
are cheap but unreliable.

So I built a service: fixed-price ($5k-12k), fixed-timeline (2-4 weeks)
MVP development. I've already shipped 3 products - Laju.dev,
Velist.dev, and Antre.in - with a combined 258k requests per second.

I'm the right person to build this because I've been the senior
engineer for 20+ startups. I know what production-ready looks like.

We're profitable, growing, and ready to scale. We'd love to join
YC to accelerate.
```

**Tips:**
- Be natural (don't read from script)
- Show, don't tell (demo your product)
- Keep it under 2 minutes
- Good audio/video quality

## 🚨 Common Mistakes

### ❌ **Don't:**
- Lie about traction (YC will find out)
- Apply with just an idea (need users/revenue)
- Say "we're the next Uber" (delusional)
- Blame competitors (focus on your strengths)
- Use jargon (keep it simple)
- Skip the video (required)
- Apply part-time (YC wants full-time founders)

### ✅ **Do:**
- Be honest (even if numbers are small)
- Show momentum (growth, even if from 0)
- Be specific (not "huge market" but "X million TAM")
- Demonstrate grit (what hard things have you done?)
- Keep it simple (grandma should understand)
- Submit early (not last minute)
- Be coachable (open to feedback)

## 📊 Traction Benchmarks

### **For YC Acceptance:**

| Metric | Minimum | Competitive | Strong |
|--------|---------|-------------|--------|
| **MRR** | $1k+ | $5k+ | $20k+ |
| **Users** | 10+ | 100+ | 1000+ |
| **Growth** | 10%/week | 20%/week | 50%/week |
| **Team** | 1 founder | 2 founders | 2-3 founders |
| **Time on idea** | 3+ months | 6+ months | 12+ months |

**Note:** These are guidelines, not rules. YC has funded companies with less traction (and rejected companies with more).

**What matters most:**
- Founder quality (can you execute?)
- Market size (is this a big opportunity?)
- Insight (do you understand something others don't?)

## 🎯 Post-Startup School

### **If Accepted to YC:**

- [ ] Prepare for program (quit job, relocate if needed)
- [ ] Set goals for 3 months
- [ ] Line up advisor network
- [ ] Prepare for Demo Day (6 weeks before)

### **If Not Accepted:**

- [ ] Don't take it personally (1.5% acceptance rate)
- [ ] Ask for feedback (if rejected after interview)
- [ ] Keep building (traction > YC stamp)
- [ ] Reapply next batch (many got in on 2nd try)
- [ ] Consider alternative accelerators (Techstars, 500, etc.)

**Remember:** YC is not the only path. Many successful companies never went through YC.

## 💬 Founder Matching

### **If Seeking Co-founder:**

**Your profile:**
```
Technical Founder (11+ years engineering)
• Built Laju.dev (258k req/sec), Velist.dev, Antre.in
• Expertise: Full-stack development, MVP delivery
• Looking for: Business/sales co-founder
• Ideal: Ex-founder, sales background, hungry to win
• Equity split: 50/50 (equal co-founders)
```

**What to look for:**
- Complementary skills (sales, marketing, ops)
- Same level of commitment (full-time, all-in)
- Shared vision (same goals, same timeline)
- Grit (won't quit when things get hard)

**Red flags in co-founder search:**
- "I have ideas but can't code" (you're not committed)
- "Looking for technical co-founder to build my idea" (you're not a builder)
- "Part-time for now" (not all-in)
- "Need NDA before sharing idea" (amateur)
