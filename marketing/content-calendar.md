### **Day 11 (Thursday)**
**Twitter Thread:** Red flags when hiring devs
```
🧵 Red flags when hiring a developer for your MVP

🚩 "I can build anything"
→ Specialist > Generalist

🚩 "I'll work for equity"
→ They don't believe in your idea

🚩 "No need for contract"
→ Amateur. Or scammer.

🚩 "I'll give you 100% upfront discount"
→ Desperate. Or inexperienced.

🚩 "I use latest tech for everything"
→ They experiment on your dime.

🚩 "4 weeks? I can do 1 week!"
→ They don't understand scope.

🚩 No portfolio of live products
→ They haven't shipped real things.

🚩 Can't explain technical tradeoffs
→ They copy-paste, don't understand.

Green flags:
✅ Asks about YOUR business goals
✅ Pushes back on scope
✅ Explains WHY, not just HOW
✅ Has live products you can test
✅ Clear about what's NOT included

Due diligence > Cheap price.
```

## 📝 Content Templates

### **Tweet Formula:**
```
Hook (controversial/interesting)
↓
Value (tip/insight/story)
↓
Proof (example/data)
↓
CTA (bookmark/follow/DM)
```

### **Thread Formula:**
```
Tweet 1: Hook + promise
Tweet 2-7: Value bombs
Tweet 8: Summary + CTA
```

### **LinkedIn Post:**
```
Same as Twitter, but:
- More professional tone
- Longer paragraphs ok
- Add "Thoughts?" at end
- Tag relevant people/companies
```

## 🎯 Daily Routine (30-60 min)

**Morning (15 min):**
- Post 1 tweet
- Reply to 5 tweets from founders
- Check DMs, respond

**Afternoon (15 min):**
- Reply to 5 more tweets
- Engage with comments on your posts
- Share 1 relevant post from others

**Evening (30 min):**
- Write tomorrow's content
- Send 5-10 cold emails
- Post on LinkedIn/Indie Hackers

**Weekly (2 hours):**
- Write 1 long-form thread
- Review analytics (what worked?)
- Plan next week's content
